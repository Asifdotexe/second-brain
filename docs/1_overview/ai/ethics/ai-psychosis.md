# AI Psychosis

When the global financial market completely loses its mind and triggers a massive, panicked selloff based entirely on a hypothetical sci-fi prediction about what an AI *might* do in three years, completely ignoring the reality of today.

**AI Psychosis** is a very recent, very weird socio-economic phenomenon where the sheer anxiety surrounding the theoretical future of artificial intelligence triggers severe, disproportionate real-world consequences in the present out of pure speculative terror.

Because we are currently living in a massive grey area—where a sci-fi future is clearly arriving but it's completely uneven and unpredictable—the global financial system is intensely, violently jittery. Even loosely substantiated thought experiments from tech bloggers or analysts are enough to trigger massive stock market crashes before the AI actually even exists.

## The 2026 Alap Shah Incident

The absolute textbook example of AI Psychosis happened in early 2026. A financial analyst named Alap Shah and his research firm published what they called a "thought exercise" titled *The 2028 Global Intelligence Crisis*.

The provocative report predicted that by the summer of 2028, everyone on earth would have dozens of personal [[agentic-ai|Agentic AI]] assistants on their phones. Shah theorized that these hyper-smart agents would totally bypass massive "middleman" corporations (like DoorDash or heavy logistics software) and aggressively negotiate prices directly with the actual local restaurants or suppliers. He predicted mass corporate bloodbaths and 10% global unemployment.

Almost no serious economist agreed with the report's timeline. AI had absolutely not caused total labor substitution yet. The technology wasn't even close to doing what Shah described.

It didn't matter. The mere publication of the report slammed into Wall Street with the force of a train. Panic immediately set in, leading to a brutal 800-point total collapse in the Dow Jones as terrified investors dumped billions of dollars of logistics and aggregator stocks out of pure, unadulterated fear of a future that didn't exist yet.

## The Root Fears

*   **The Doom Loop:** The core terror driving AI Psychosis is a negative economic death spiral: AI takes jobs $\rightarrow$ normal people have less money $\rightarrow$ consumer spending collapses $\rightarrow$ corporations panic and conduct massive layoffs $\rightarrow$ repeat.
*   **Death to the Middlemen:** The stock market seems most violently allergic to the idea that [[agentic-ai|Autonomous Agents]] will achieve zero-friction direct contracting. If an AI can perfectly negotiate a car rental directly with the owner, the multi-billion-dollar app sitting in the middle managing the marketplace instantly becomes worthless.
