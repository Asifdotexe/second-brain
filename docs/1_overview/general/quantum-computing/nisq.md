# NISQ (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum)

The "Prototype Era" ,the current stage of quantum development where we have computers that are powerful enough to be useful, but "noisy" enough to be extremely annoying and error-prone.

**NISQ** (pronounced "nisk") is an acronym coined by physicist John Preskill to describe the technology we have *right now*. A NISQ computer is a machine with 50 to 500 **[[qubits|Qubits]]** that lacks the "Error Correction" needed to run massive, perfect calculations.

Think of a NISQ computer like a **Vintage Race Car**:
*   It is incredibly fast ,it can reach speeds a normal car could never dream of.
*   **BUT**, the wheels are constantly wobbling, the engine is smoking, and parts fall off every five minutes.
*   You can't use it to drive your family across the country (Long Calculations), but you can use it for **short, intense test laps** (Small Math Problems) to learn how to build a better car for the future.

## Why is it "Noisy"?
In the quantum world, "Noise" is the enemy. Because **[[qubits|Qubits]]** are so tiny and sensitive, the slightest change in temperature, the vibration from a passing truck, or even a stray Wi-Fi signal can cause the Qubit to "lose its mind" (**Decoherence**). This introduces errors into the math, making the results fuzzy or "noisy."

## Why Use It If It’s Broken?
Even "noisy" quantum computers can do things that a regular **[[supercomputer|Supercomputer]]** cannot. Researchers use NISQ devices for:
1.  **Chemical Simulations:** Designing new batteries or medicines by simulating how atoms dance together.
2.  **Optimization:** Finding the perfect route for 10,000 delivery trucks or the most efficient way to pack a shipping container.
3.  **[[quantum-machine-learning|Quantum AI]]:** Using the "fuzziness" of quantum math to help artificial intelligence recognize patterns faster.

## FAQs

*1. When will the NISQ era end?*
The goal is **"Fault-Tolerant Quantum Computing."** This will happen when we have millions of physical qubits working together to fix their own mistakes. Most experts think we are at least **5 to 10 years** away from leaving the NISQ era.

*2. Who owns these NISQ computers?*
Mostly the tech giants and universities. **Google**, **IBM**, and companies like **IonQ** or **Rigetti** are the leaders in building these "beautiful, noisy prototypes."
