# Cryptomining Software

The "Digital Pickaxe"—specialized programs designed to solve incredibly difficult mathematical puzzles to help secure a blockchain and earn new cryptocurrency in return.

**Cryptomining software** is the engine of the crypto world. It turns your computer's "Processing Power" into a security tool. In exchange for using your electricity to solve these math puzzles, the network (like Bitcoin or Monero) rewards you with a small amount of "Digital Gold."

Think of it like **A Global Lottery**:
*   To win the lottery, you have to buy a ticket. 
*   In the crypto world, "Buying a ticket" means your computer solved a complex math puzzle. 
*   The faster and more powerful your computer, the more "tickets" you can buy every second, increasing your chances of winning the reward.

## The Good vs. The Ugly

1.  **The Legitimate Miner:** This is a hobbyist or a business. They buy expensive **[[gpu|GPUs]]** or specialized **[[hardware|ASICs]]**, they pay their own electricity bills, and they run the software (like *NiceHash*) intentionally to make a profit.
2.  **The [[cryptojacking|Cryptojacker]] (The Thief):** This is a hacker who secretly installs mining software on **other people's** computers. Because mining uses massive amounts of power, the hacker gets the "Reward" while the victim gets a slow computer and a massive electric bill.

## Why is it Heavy?
Cryptomining is "100% Effort." The software tells your **[[cpu|CPU]]** or **[[gpu|GPU]]** to work as hard as it possibly can, non-stop. This has three big side effects:
*   **Heat:** Your computer will get very hot, very fast.
*   **Lag:** Because the miner is using 100% of the computer's brain, your web browser or video games will feel slow and laggy.
*   **Wear and Tear:** Running your "Engine" at max speed 24/7 will eventually shorten the life of your hardware.

## FAQs

*1. Is it a virus?*
**By itself, no.** However, because hackers love to hide it in other files, most **[[antivirus|Antivirus]]** software will flag it as a "Potentially Unwanted Program" (PUP). If you didn't install it yourself, it is definitely a threat.

*2. Can my laptop mine Bitcoin?*
Technically, yes, but it’s a waste of time. These days, "Professional" miners use specialized machines that are 10,000x faster than a laptop. Mining Bitcoin on a laptop would likely cost you $10 in electricity to earn less than 1 cent of Bitcoin.
