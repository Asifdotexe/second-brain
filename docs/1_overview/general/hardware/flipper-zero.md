# Flipper Zero

The "Hacker’s Tamagotchi" ,a portable, multi-tool "Swiss Army Knife" for geeks that can talk to almost every invisible radio wave in the world, from garage door openers to office keycards.

The **Flipper Zero** is a device that looks like a toy from the 90s, complete with a pixelated digital dolphin that lives inside. But behind that cute screen is a powerhouse of antennas. It can read, record, and "replay" signals for **[[nfc|NFC]]**, **[[rfid|RFID]]**, **Infrared** (TV remotes), and **Sub-GHz** (wireless keys).

Think of it as a **Universal Translator for Invisible Things**:
*   Normally, your phone only speaks "Bluetooth" and "Wi-Fi."
*   The Flipper speaks "Hotel Key," "Garage Door," "TV Remote," and "Gas Station Price Sign." It captures these invisible "conversations" from the air and lets you interact with them.

## Is it a "Weapon"?
The Flipper Zero became famous (and infamous) on TikTok for people using it to do "pranks," like turning off TVs in restaurants or opening Tesla charge ports. 
*   **The Reality:** It is a **Learning Tool**. It helps security researchers and hobbyists understand how their world works. Proving that a garage door is "vulnerable" to a Flipper helps the company build a better, safer door next time.

## What Can it Actually Do?

*   **Remote Control:** It can clone your TV remote or your AC remote so you have one device to rule them all.
*   **Access Control:** It can "Backup" your office badge or your apartment keycard so if you lose yours, you have a spare.
*   **Bad Stuff (Don't do this):** It was used to perform "Bluetooth Spam" attacks that could crash an iPhone by flooding it with fake pairing requests (most of these "holes" have since been patched).

## FAQs

*1. Is it illegal to own one?*
**No.** The device itself is completely legal to buy and own. However, **using it** to interact with things you don't own (like opening a neighbor's garage or cloning a stranger's credit card) is a crime. Use it for your own stuff to learn how hardware works!

*2. Why is there a Dolphin in it?*
The dolphin is your companion. As you use the device to "hack" things and explore protocols, the dolphin levels up and grows, just like a Tamagotchi. It’s a fun way to "Gamify" learning about cybersecurity.
