# NFC (Near Field Communication)

The "Digital Handshake" ,a super-secure, short-range wireless technology that allows two devices to share information only when they are practically touching each other.

**NFC** is the technology that makes "Tap to Pay" possible. It is a specialized version of **[[rfid|RFID]]** technology. The key "secret" of NFC is its range: it only works if devices are within **4 centimeters** (about 1.5 inches) of each other. If you are any further away, the signal vanishes. This tiny range is exactly what makes it so safe for your money.

Think of it like a **Quiet Whisper**:
*   **Wi-Fi/Bluetooth:** Is like shouting across a room. Anyone nearby can hear you if they try hard enough.
*   **NFC:** Is like leaning in and whispering a secret directly into someone’s ear. Unless they are literally touching you, they can't hear a word.

## The 3 Ways We Use NFC

1.  **The Wallet (Apple/Google Pay):** Your phone "pretends" to be a credit card. When you tap it on a terminal, the NFC chip sends an encrypted "Handshake" to the bank to confirm the payment without ever sharing your actual card number.
2.  **The Key (Access Cards):** Many modern office buildings use NFC. Your phone or a plastic badge acts as a key that "unlocks" the door the moment it touches the reader.
3.  **The Shortcut (NFC Tags):** You can buy tiny, $1 stickers called "NFC Tags." You can stick one on your nightstand and program it so that when you tap your phone on it, your lights turn off and your alarm is set automatically.

## FAQs

*1. Can a hacker steal my money by walking past me?*
In theory, yes ("Skimming"). In practice, **no.** They would have to get their bulky reader within 1 inch of your pocket without you noticing. Even then, modern phones require you to "Unlock" with your face or fingerprint before the NFC chip will even turn on. It is significantly safer than a traditional "Swipe" credit card.

*2. Does NFC use my battery?*
**Barely at all.** NFC is "Passive." This means that the NFC chip in your phone or card doesn't even need its own power to work; it "wakes up" only when it feels the tiny magnetic field of the payment terminal.
