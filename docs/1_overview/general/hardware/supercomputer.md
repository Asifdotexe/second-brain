# Supercomputer

The "Formula 1 Car" of the digital world—a massive, room-sized machine made of thousands of processors working in perfect harmony to solve the world’s most impossible math problems.

A **Supercomputer** isn't just a "really fast version" of your laptop. It is built using an entirely different philosophy called **Parallel Processing**. While your laptop is a "Generalist" (it’s good at email, music, and web browsing all at once), a Supercomputer is a "Specialist." It is built to take one single, gargantuan problem—like simulating the birth of a galaxy or predicting a hurricane—and break it into a million tiny pieces that it solves simultaneously.

Think of it like **Reading a Massive Library**:
*   **A Fast Computer:** Is one genius who can read a whole book in 1 second. It will still take them hours to finish the library.
*   **A Supercomputer:** Is 10,000 average people. Each person is given **one page** of the library. They all read their page at the exact same time. The entire library is finished in a single blink of an eye.

## The King of Math: The FLOP
We don't measure Supercomputers in "Gigahertz." We measure them in **FLOPS** (Floating Point Operations Per Second).
*   **Current Champion:** **Frontier** (located at Oak Ridge National Lab in the USA).
*   **The Speed:** It achieved "Exascale" speed—**1.1 ExaFLOPS**. That is 1.1 quintillion calculations every single second. To match what Frontier does in one second, every human on Earth would have to do one calculation per second for four years.

## FAQs

*1. Does it run Windows or Mac?*
**Neither.** Almost 100% of the world's fastest supercomputers run **[[operating-system|Linux]]**. Linux is the only operating system flexible enough to manage 10,000 processors and 100 miles of internal cabling without crashing.

*2. Can I use one to play video games?*
Technically, yes, but it would be a terrible experience. Supercomputers are designed for "Throughput" (massive math), not "Latency" (quick responses to your mouse click). Using a Supercomputer to play Minecraft is like using a massive NASA rocket to drive to the grocery store—it’s the wrong tool for the job.
