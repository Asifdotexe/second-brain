# NPU (Neural Processing Unit)

The "Instinctive Brain"—a tiny, hyper-efficient chip inside your phone or laptop that is designed to do only one thing: run Artificial Intelligence instantly while using almost zero battery.

An **NPU** is a specialist. While your **[[cpu|CPU]]** handles the thinking and your **[[gpu|GPU]]** handles the visuals, the NPU handles the **AI Features**. In a modern smartphone, the NPU is the reason why your camera can recognize a face, why your battery lasts all day despite running complex apps, and why Siri can understand you even when you're whispering.

Think of it as **Reflexes vs. Intelligence**:
*   **The [[tpu|TPU]] (Cloud AI):** Is like a scientist in a lab solving a complex math problem. It’s powerful, but it takes a long time and uses a lot of energy.
*   **The NPU (On-Device AI):** Is like your hand pulling away from a hot stove. It’s not "smart" in a general way, but it is **instant**. It happens right there, on the device in your hand, without needing the internet.

## Why Do We Need a Third Chip?

1.  **Privacy:** Because the NPU is on your phone, your face data (FaceID) or your voice never has to leave your device. It doesn't go to a server in the cloud; it stays in your pocket.
2.  **Speed:** Sending data to the cloud takes time (Lag). The NPU does its work in milliseconds.
3.  **Battery Life:** This is the most important part. Running an AI model on a **[[cpu|CPU]]** would drain your phone's battery in 30 minutes. An NPU is "hard-wired" for this math, meaning it can run AI features all day while barely using 1% of your battery.

## FAQs

*1. Do I have an NPU?*
If you have a modern smartphone (like an iPhone with the "Apple Neural Engine" or a Samsung with "Hexagon"), **yes**. Every time you blur the background in a video call or use "Portrait Mode," you are using your NPU.

*2. Is an NPU the same as a GPU?*
Not exactly. A **[[gpu|GPU]]** is amazing at *teaching* a machine (Training). An NPU is amazing at *using* what the machine learned (Inference). The NPU is like the "Player" who uses the playbook during the game.
