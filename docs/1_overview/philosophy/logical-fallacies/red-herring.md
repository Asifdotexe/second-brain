# Red Herring

"Look, a Squirrel!" ,a logical fallacy where someone introduces a completely irrelevant topic into a debate to distract everyone from the original, difficult question.

**A Red Herring** is the "Smoke Bomb" of arguments. When someone is losing or doesn't want to answer a tough question, they throw a "Red Herring" into the room. It’s usually an emotional or controversial topic that makes people forget what they were originally talking about.

Think of it like **Scented Fish**:
*   The name comes from an old legend about escaped prisoners. To stop tracking dogs from following their scent, they would drag a pungent, smelly fish (a Red Herring) across their trail.
*   The dogs would smell the strong fish, get confused, and follow the new scent, allowing the prisoner to escape.
*   In a debate, the "Smelly Fish" is a topic like *"But what about the flag?"* or *"What about the children?"* tossed into a boring conversation about tax codes.

## How to Spot It
1.  **Person A asks a tough question:** "Why did you use company money to buy a boat?"
2.  **Person B throws the Red Herring:** "The real question is, why do people in this town hate successful businessmen? I’ve worked here for 30 years and I love this community!"
3.  **The Result:** If Person A starts arguing about how much they "love the community," the Red Herring worked. The boat is forgotten.

## FAQs

*1. Is it the same as a [[straw-man|Straw Man]]?*
**No.** 
*   **[[straw-man|Straw Man]]:** Twists your actual argument to make it look stupid.
*   **Red Herring:** Changes the subject entirely to avoid the argument.

*2. Is "Whataboutism" a Red Herring?*
**Yes.** It is a specific type. *"You stole my car!"* -> *"But what about that time you forgot my birthday?"* Your birthday has nothing to do with the stolen car ,it is a Red Herring used to make you feel guilty and stop the accusation.
