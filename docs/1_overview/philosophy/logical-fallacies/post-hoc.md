# Post Hoc Fallacy

"The Rain Dancer"—a logical error where you assume that because Event B happened after Event A, then Event A must have **caused** Event B.

**Post Hoc** (short for *Post Hoc Ergo Propter Hoc*, Latin for "After this, therefore because of this") is the birth of almost every superstition in history. Our brains are "Pattern-Matching Machines." We are evolved to find causes for everything, but we often mistake a simple **Coincidence** for a powerful **Cause**.

Think of it like **Lucky Socks**:
*   **The Event:** You wore your lucky socks. Then, your favorite sports team won the championship.
*   **The Fallacy:** *"My socks caused the win! If I don't wear them next time, they will lose!"*
*   **The Reality:** The team won because they practiced, had better players, and played well. Your socks stayed in your shoes the whole time and had zero impact on the physics of the game.

## Correlation $\neq$ Causation
This is the most important rule in science. Just because two things change at the same time doesn't mean they are related. 
*   *Example:* During the Summer, ice cream sales go up. Also, during the Summer, murder rates go up. 
*   **The Fallacy:** *"Ice cream causes murder!"*
*   **The Reality:** The **Heat** causes both. People eat more ice cream when it's hot, and people get more aggressive when it's hot.

## FAQs

*1. Why do we keep falling for this?*
Because it was a survival skill. In the ancient jungle, if you ate a blue berry and then got a stomach ache, assuming "Berry caused Ache" kept you alive. Our brains prefer a "False Positive" (being superstitious) over a "False Negative" (dying because we ignored a pattern).

*2. How do I avoid it?*
Ask for the **Mechanism**. If someone says A caused B, ask them exactly **How**. If there is no logical physical link (like socks affecting an athlete miles away), it is likely just a coincidence.
