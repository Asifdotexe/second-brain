# Middle Ground Fallacy

"The Truth is in the Middle" ,a logical error that assumes the "correct" answer to a problem is always the compromise halfway between two opposing views.

**The Middle Ground Fallacy** (formally *Argumentum ad Temperantiam*) sounds very reasonable. We are taught from a young age that "Compromise is Good." However, logic doesn't care about fairness. Sometimes, one side is 100% correct and the other is 100% wrong. In those cases, the "Middle Ground" is just a watered-down lie.

Think of it like **Drinking Poison**:
*   **Person A:** "You should drink a gallon of deadly poison."
*   **Person B:** "You should drink zero poison."
*   **The Fallacy:** "Clearly, the truth is in the middle. We should compromise and drink **half a gallon** of poison."
*   **The Reality:** Person B is 100% correct. Half a gallon of poison is still a terrible idea.

## Why it Happens
1.  **Conflict Avoidance:** People hate arguing. Splitting the difference feels like a "Win-Win" that lets everyone go home happy.
2.  **Laziness:** It’s easier to pick the middle than it is to actually study the data and decide who is right.
3.  **The "Both Sides" Bias:** News programs often feel they must have one person from "Side A" and one from "Side B," giving them equal time and status even if one side is a world-class expert and the other is a random person with no evidence.

## FAQs

*1. Is compromise ever good?*
**Yes!** Compromise is great for **Actions** and **Relationships**. If you want Pizza and your friend wants Sushi, the "Middle Ground" (maybe an Italian-Asian fusion place?) is a great social win. But for **Facts** (like "Does gravity work?"), there is no compromise.

*2. What is the opposite of this?*
The **[[false-dilemma|False Dilemma]]**, which is the belief that there are **only** two extremes and no middle ground at all.
