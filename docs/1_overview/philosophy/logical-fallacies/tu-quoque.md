# Tu Quoque

"You Too!" ,a logical fallacy where you reject someone’s argument simply because they are a hypocrite who doesn't follow their own advice.

**Tu Quoque** (Latin for "You also") is one of the most common ways people deflect criticism. Instead of answering the question, they point a finger back and say: *"How can you lecture me? You do the same thing!"* While they might be right about the hypocrisy, the hypocrisy has **nothing to do** with whether the original advice was correct.

Think of it like **The Smoking Doctor**:
*   A doctor tells you: *"Smoking will destroy your lungs. Here are 50 studies showing that it causes cancer. You should quit today."*
*   As he says this, he pulls a pack of cigarettes out of his pocket and starts smoking.
*   **The Fallacy:** *"You smoke! Clearly, your lung cancer data is a lie. I'm going to keep smoking."*
*   **The Reality:** The doctor is a hypocrite and a bad role model. But the **data** is still 100% true. His personal habit doesn't change the biology of your lungs.

## How it Appears in Real Life
*   **Parents:** "Don't lie to me." -> **Child:** "But you lied to the waiter about my age to get a discount! So I can lie too!" (The parent is a hypocrite, but "Lying is bad" is still a valid rule).
*   **Politics:** "Your party is corrupt!" -> **Response:** "Well, your party was corrupt back in 1999!" (This doesn't prove the first party isn't corrupt; it just changes the subject to an old grudge).

## FAQs

*1. Does hypocrisy ever matter?*
**Yes, for Trust.** If you are picking a leader or a friend, hypocrisy is a huge red flag for their character. But in a **Logical Argument**, hypocrisy is irrelevant. A thief who tells you "Stealing is illegal" is 100% correct about the law, even if he has a stolen watch in his pocket.

*2. How is this different from [[ad-hominem|Ad Hominem]]?*
A **Tu Quoque** is a specific type of Ad Hominem. All Tu Quoques are personal attacks, but specifically ones that focus on the "Double Standard."
