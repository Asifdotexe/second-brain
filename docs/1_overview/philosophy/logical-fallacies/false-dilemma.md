# False Dilemma

Black and White.

**The False Dilemma** (or False Dichotomy) is a fallacy where a complex situation is presented as having only two options; usually "My Way" or "The Apocalypse"â€”when in reality there are many middle grounds.

Think of it like **A Light Switch vs. A Dimmer**.
*   **The Fallacy:** "The room is either completely dark or blindingly bright!" (A Switch).
*   **The Reality:** Most rooms have dimmer switches. You can have 50% brightness, 20% brightness, or candle light.

## How it Works

1.  **Over-Simplification:** "You are either with us, or against us."
2.  **Forced Choice:** This trap forces the opponent to defend an extreme position they don't actually hold, or accept your extreme position.
3.  **Ignoring Nuance:** It erases compromise, context, and third options.

## FAQs

*1. Is it always a fallacy?*
No. Sometimes there *are* only two options. "You are either pregnant or you are not." There is no "kind of" pregnant. But in politics and ethics, binary choices are rare.

*2. Why is it used?*
It creates urgency and fear. Salespeople use it ("Buy now or lose this deal forever!") to bypass critical thinking.
