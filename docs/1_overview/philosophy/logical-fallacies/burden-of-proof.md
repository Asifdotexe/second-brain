# The Burden of Proof

"Prove It!" ,the logical rule that says if you make a claim, it is **your** job to provide the evidence. It is never the job of the person who doesn't believe you to prove you wrong.

**The Burden of Proof** (formally called *Onus Probandi*) is the foundation of science, law, and fair debate. Without it, anyone could claim anything ("The moon is made of blue cheese!") and then demand that you spend your whole life proving them wrong. In a logical world, the person bringing the "New Idea" must bring the "New Data."

Think of it like **A Courtroom**:
*   A Prosecutor stands up and points at a man: *"That man stole my car!"*
*   **The Fallacy:** The Prosecutor then sits down and says to the man, *"Now, prove that you didn't steal it!"*
*   **The Rule:** The Judge will laugh and throw the case out. The "Burden" is on the Prosecutor to show footprints, video tape, or DNA. Until they do, the man is "Innocent" by default.

## The "Null Hypothesis"
In science, we always start with the **Null Hypothesis**, which is the assumption that "Nothing is happening" or "This thing doesn't exist." 
*   We don't assume a new pill cures cancer until we see the data. 
*   We don't assume aliens are in the room until we see them. 
*   The Burden of Proof is the energy required to "move" the world away from the Null Hypothesis.

## FAQs

*1. What is "Hitchens’s Razor"?*
Named after the writer Christopher Hitchens, it is the ultimate defense against people who try to skip the Burden of Proof: **"What can be asserted without evidence can also be dismissed without evidence."** If you bring nothing to the table, I don't have to bring anything to the debate to ignore you.

*2. What if both people make a claim?*
Then **both** have a burden. If you say, *"The economy will crash tomorrow,"* and your friend says, *"The economy will boom tomorrow,"* you both have to show your work. Neither of you gets a "free pass."
