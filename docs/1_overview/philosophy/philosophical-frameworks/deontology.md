# Deontology

"The Rule is the Rule"—an ethical framework that says an action is right or wrong based on a set of rules, regardless of what the final outcome is.

**Deontology** (from the Greek word *deon*, meaning "duty") is the philosophy of moral rules. It says that you have a duty to do certain things (like being honest) and a duty *not* to do others (like stealing). Even if breaking a rule would make everyone happier, a Deontologist says you still shouldn't do it because the **Action** itself is what matters, not the **Result**.

Think of it like **Playing a Video Game with a Referee**:
*   **[[utilitarianism|Utilitarianism]]:** Says the point of the game is to get the highest score possible, even if you have to cheat a little bit.
*   **Deontology:** Says the point of the game is to **Follow the Rules**. If you follow the rules perfectly and still lose the game, you are still "Moral." If you win by cheating, you are "Immoral," no matter how high your score is.

## The Test: The Categorical Imperative
Immanuel Kant, the giant of deontology, created a simple test: **"Would it be okay if *everyone* did what I am about to do?"**
*   **Lying?** If everyone lied, human language would become useless. No one would believe anyone. Therefore, you must **never** lie.
*   **Stealing?** If everyone stole, the concept of "Property" would vanish. Therefore, you must **never** steal.

## FAQs

*1. What if lying saves a life?*
This is the famous "Murderer at the door" problem. If a killer asks if your friend is hiding in your house:
*   **The Utilitarian:** Says "Lie!" (1 life saved is better than 1 lie).
*   **The Deontologist:** Says "Tell the truth." Lying is a sin. If the killer kills your friend, that is the killer's sin, not yours. You did your duty by being honest. (Most people find this part of Deontology very difficult to accept).

*2. Why use this?*
Because it provides **certainty**. You don't have to be a psychic or a mathematician to be a good person. You don't have to guess the future. You just have to know the rules and follow them.
