# Stoicism

"The Mental Shield"—a user's manual for the mind that teaches you how to be happy by focusing intensely on what you can control, and completely letting go of what you can't.

**Stoicism** was built by a diverse group: a Greek slave (Epictetus), a Roman statesman (Seneca), and a Roman Emperor (Marcus Aurelius). They all agreed on one thing: Life is full of chaos and pain. You cannot stop the world from being difficult, but you can build a "Fortress" inside your own mind that no one can break.

Think of it like **Navigating a Storm**:
*   You are the captain of a ship. You **cannot** control the wind, the waves, or the lightning. If you spend your time screaming at the clouds, you will drown.
*   The only things you **can** control are the rudder, the sails, and your own courage.
*   The Stoic ignores the storm and focuses entirely on the rudder. If the ship sinks, the Stoic dies with their dignity intact, knowing they did their job perfectly.

## The "Dichotomy of Control"
This is the most important rule in Stoicism. Draw a line in the sand:
*   **Inside Your Control:** Your thoughts, your reactions, how you treat people, and your own effort.
*   **Outside Your Control:** The weather, the economy, traffic, what other people think of you, and the past.
*   **The Result:** If you tie your happiness to things outside the line (like "I hope my boss likes me"), you are a slave. If you tie it to things inside the line (like "I will do my best work"), you are a King.

## FAQs

*1. Does it mean I have to be a "Robot" with no feelings?*
**No.** Striving for "Stoic Calm" doesn't mean you don't feel love, joy, or grief. It means you don't let those feelings **Enslave** you. You can feel a wave of anger, but a Stoic chooses not to let that anger make them say something they’ll regret.

*2. How do I start?*
Follow the example of Marcus Aurelius. He wrote a diary called *Meditations* (which was never meant to be published). In it, he reminded himself every morning: *"The people I meet today will be annoying, ungrateful, and selfish. I cannot be hurt by them, because I will not let them change my character."*
