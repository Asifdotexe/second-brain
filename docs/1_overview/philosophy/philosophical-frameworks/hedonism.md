# Hedonism

"The Pleasure Compass" ,the simple belief that Happiness (Pleasure) is the only thing that is truly "Good," and Suffering (Pain) is the only thing that is truly "Bad."

**Hedonism** is often misunderstood as just "partying and excess." But at its core, it is an ethical system. A Hedonist asks one question before every decision: *"Will this action result in the most pleasure and the least pain?"* If yes, then the action is moral.

Think of it like **Investing in Happiness**:
*   **The Wild Hedonist (Cyrenaics):** Is like someone who spends their whole paycheck on a massive party tonight. They want the highest "High" right now, even if they are broke tomorrow.
*   **The Calm Hedonist (Epicureans):** Is like a smart investor. They realize that a "Hangover" is a form of pain. So, they prefer a quiet dinner with good friends and a simple meal because it provides **long-term happiness** without the "Market Crash" of a headache the next morning.

## The Two Paths of Pleasure

1.  **Instant Pleasure (Cyrenaics):** Physical sensations. The taste of a burger, the feeling of the sun, the rush of a win. "Eat, drink, and be merry, for tomorrow we die."
2.  **Absence of Pain (Epicureanism):** Peace of mind. Epicurus argued that the highest pleasure isn't a "High," but rather the **Freedom from Anxiety**. A simple life with no stress is the ultimate hedonistic win.

## FAQs

*1. Is it selfish?*
**Frequently, yes.** But **[[utilitarianism|Utilitarianism]]** is a form of "Social Hedonism." It says we should maximize the pleasure of **everyone** in society, not just ourselves. "The greatest good for the greatest number."

*2. Why does it eventually stop working? (The Treadmill)*
Psychologists call this the **"Hedonic Treadmill."** When you buy a nice new car, you get a spike of pleasure. But after a month, it just becomes "your car." You return to your normal baseline, and you need a *faster* car to get the same hit. Chasing pleasure can become an infinite race where you never actually move.
