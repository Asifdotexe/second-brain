# Cynicism

"Living Like a Dog"—an ancient philosophy of rejecting all social rules, fame, and wealth to find true, unshakeable freedom through nature and simplicity.

Modern people think a "Cynic" is someone who expects the worst from everyone. But **Ancient Cynicism** was much more radical. It was the belief that the "civilized" world (money, manners, status) is a trap. To be truly happy, you must reject everything the world tells you to care about and live like an animal—shameless, honest, and free.

Think of it like **A Stray Dog**:
A dog doesn't care if it wears a designer collar. A dog doesn't care if people applaud when it walks by. A dog just wants sunlight, food, and a place to sleep. If you are as simple as a dog, nobody can "fire" you, nobody can "insult" you, and nobody can take your wealth. You are invincible because you own nothing.

## The Madman: Diogenes
The most famous Cynic was Diogenes of Sinope. He lived in a ceramic tub in the middle of Athens to prove his point.
*   **Baggage:** He owned a wooden bowl until he saw a child drinking from their hands. He smashed the bowl, saying, *"A child has beaten me in simplicity."*
*   **Pride:** When Alexander the Great (the most powerful man on Earth) came to meet him and asked, *"What can I do for you?"* Diogenes replied: *"Yes, move. You are blocking my sunlight."*

## FAQs

*1. Is it the same as being a "Skeptic"?*
**No.** A skeptic doubts what people *say*. A Cynic doubts what people *value*. A Cynic looks at a billionaire and thinks, *"Why did you waste your life collecting green papers?"*

*2. How do I practice it today?*
You don't have to move into a tub. You practice it by:
*   Realizing you don't *need* the new iPhone to be happy.
*   Speaking the truth even when it’s uncomfortable.
*   Stopping yourself from caring about "Likes" or "Followers."
