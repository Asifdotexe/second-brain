# Grandfather Paradox

"The Reality Crash" ,a famous logical puzzle where a time traveler goes back in time and accidentally (or on purpose) stops themselves from ever being born, creating a loop that the universe cannot solve.

**The Grandfather Paradox** is the ultimate "Error Message" for time travel. It highlights the problem of **Causality** ,the rule that the Cause must always come before the Effect. If you destroy your own cause, you logically cannot exist to perform the action.

Think of it like **Deleting the Folder You Are Currently Using**:
*   Imagine you are using a computer. You open a folder and start a program that is inside it.
*   Once the program is running, you use that program to **Delete the Folder** that contains it.
*   **The Result:** The computer crashes. The program can't run if its "Home" is gone, but it can't delete the Home unless it is running. The logic has "Divided by Zero" and the system breaks.

## The Infinite Loop
1.  **You are born** in the year 2000.
2.  **You build a time machine** in 2024 and go back to 1920.
3.  **You kill your grandfather** before he meets your grandmother.
4.  **Result:** You are never born.
5.  **BUT:** If you are never born, nobody builds the time machine.
6.  **SO:** Nobody goes back to 1920, the grandfather lives, and you **ARE** born. (Repeat forever).

## Possible Fixes (The "Patches")
Scientists and writers have come up with ways to "save" the universe from this crash:
*   **Multiverses:** When you go back, you aren't in *your* past. You are in a "Copy" of the past. If you kill the grandfather there, you just create a new timeline where you don't exist, but your original home stays the same.
*   **Fate (Self-Consistency):** The universe simply won't let you do it. The gun will jam, or you'll accidentally kill the wrong person. History is "Fixed" and cannot be changed.
*   **A "New" Grandfather:** You discover that the man you killed wasn't actually your grandfather, and your "True" grandfather was the one who helped you hide the body.

## FAQs

*1. Is backward time travel possible?*
**Probably not.** Most physicists believe that this paradox is proof that the universe has a "No-Causality-Violation" rule. While moving **Forward** in time is easy (just go fast!), moving **Backward** would break the logic of the universe.
