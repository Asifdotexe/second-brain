# Just-So Stories

"Scientific Creative Writing"—the criticism for explanations that sound smart and logical, but are actually untestable guesses made "backwards" to fit the facts we already see.

The name comes from Rudyard Kipling's children's book *Just So Stories*, which explains things like "How the Elephant Got Its Trunk" (a crocodile pulled on it). In science, a **Just-So Story** is when a researcher looks at a modern human behavior (like "Men liking the color blue") and invents a prehistoric "Reason" for it without any actual evidence.

Think of it like **Retrofitting a Crime Scene**:
*   **Real Science:** You find a footprint (Evidence), predict who it belongs to (Hypothesis), and then go find that person's shoes to check (Test).
*   **A Just-So Story:** You decide you hate a certain person. You then "Invent" a story about how they could have teleported into the room, even though you have zero proof of teleportation. You say, *"See? The story fits the fact that the door was locked!"*
*   **The Problem:** The story fits because you **built the story to fit the result.** You are working backwards from the answer.

## The Evolution Trap
Just-So Stories are most common in **Evolutionary Psychology**. 
*   **The Story:** *"Men like fire because prehistoric men had to sit around campfires to protect their families."*
*   **The Reality:** It sounds plausible! But how do you prove it? You can't. You don't have a time machine. Unless you can make a **[[novel-prediction|Novel Prediction]]** (e.g., *"If this fire story is true, we should see this specific protein change in the brain"*), it’s just a bedtime story, not a scientific theory.

## FAQs

*1. How do I spot a Just-So Story?*
**Ask for the "Falsifier."** Ask the person: *"Is there any evidence you could find that would prove your story is WRONG?"* If they say "No, it just makes sense," then it’s a Just-So Story.

*2. Does this mean the explanation is wrong?*
**No.** The story might be true! But it isn't "Science" yet. Until you can test it and potentially prove it wrong, it remains a "Creative Guess."
