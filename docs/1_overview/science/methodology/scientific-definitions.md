# Scientific Definitions

"The Hierarchy of Truth"—precise labels for different levels of knowledge. In daily life, people use the word "Theory" as an insult. In science, calling something a "Theory" is the highest honor it can receive.

In everyday English, "Theory" means a hunch or a guess: *"I have a theory about why my cat is grumpy."* In science, that is called a **Hypothesis**. Mixing these up is like confusing a single brick (Fact) with the blueprint for a whole skyscraper (Theory). You need the bricks to build the building, but the blueprint is what explains how the bricks stay up.

## The 4 Levels of Knowledge

1.  **FACT: An Observation.**
    *   *What it is:* A data point that has been confirmed many times.
    *   *Example:* "The apple fell from the tree."
2.  **HYPOTHESIS: A Testable Guess.**
    *   *What it is:* A specific explanation for a fact that can be checked.
    *   *Example:* "The apple fell because an invisible force (Gravity) pulls it down."
3.  **LAW: A Mathematical Description.**
    *   *What it is:* A formula that tells you **what** will happen every time, but not **why**.
    *   *Example:* $F = ma$ (The math that calculates how fast the apple hits the ground).
4.  **THEORY: The Final Explanation.**
    *   *What it is:* The "Full Picture." A framework of facts, laws, and tested hypotheses that explains **why** things happen.
    *   *Example:* **The Theory of General Relativity** (Explains that gravity isn't just a "pull," it's the actual warping of space-time).

## FAQs

*1. Does a Theory eventually become a Law?*
**NO.** This is the biggest myth in science class. A theory is more complex than a law. A Law is just a "Description" (like a photo). A Theory is an "Explanation" (like a documentary). A photo never "Grows up" to become a documentary.

*2. Is a Theory "just a guess"?*
**Absolutely not.** A theory like **The Germ Theory of Disease** or **The Atomic Theory** is supported by millions of facts. It is the most "Solid" thing we have in science. We call it a "Theory" because we are honest—scientists admit that we are always one [[falsifiability|black swan]] away from needing to update the explanation.
