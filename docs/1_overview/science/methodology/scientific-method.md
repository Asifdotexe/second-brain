# Scientific Method

"The Universe Debugger"—the logical "Algorithm" we use to test our ideas, minimize our own biases, and move closer to the truth.

**The Scientific Method** is more of a "Mindset" than a strict 1-2-3 recipe. It is a way of saying: *"I have an idea, but I know my brain usually tricks me, so I’m going to run a test that gives my idea a chance to fail."* It is the most powerful tool humans have ever invented for cleaning up the "Messy Data" of reality. 

Think of it like **Fixing a Broken Lamp**:
1.  **Observation:** You flip the switch. The lamp doesn't turn on.
2.  **Question:** Why is it dark?
3.  **Hypothesis:** *"I bet the bulb is burnt out."* (This is a [[falsifiability|falsifiable]] guess).
4.  **Experiment:** You screw in a new bulb.
5.  **Data:** The light turns on!
6.  **Conclusion:** The old bulb was dead.
*   **The "Science" Part:** If you put in a new bulb and it **still** didn't work, you'd go back to Step 3: *"Maybe the outlet is broken?"* You keep looping until you find the truth.

## The Scientific Loop
Science doesn't have a "Finish Line." Every conclusion just starts a new question.
1.  **Observe:** "That's weird..."
2.  **Research:** "Has anyone else seen this?"
3.  **Hypothesize:** "I think it’s because of X."
4.  **Test:** "Let's try to break X."
5.  **Analyze:** "What did we learn?"
6.  **Review:** "Hey world, check my homework."

## FAQs

*1. Is it a strict 6-step process?*
**No.** In real life, science is messy. You might be halfway through an experiment and realize your original question was wrong. You might find something amazing by accident (like Penicillin) that changes your whole career. The "Steps" are just a map to make sure your logic is sound.

*2. Why is "Failing" an experiment good?*
Because in science, **Proving yourself wrong is a win.** It tells you that your guess wasn't true, which saves everyone else the time of making the same mistake. You are now one step closer to the real answer.
