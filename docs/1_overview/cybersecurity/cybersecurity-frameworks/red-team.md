# Red Team

"The Digital Saboteurs" ,a team of ethical hackers hired to act like the most dangerous criminals on the planet. They test a company's defenses by trying to break, steal, and manipulate everything in their path.

A **Red Team** challenges an organization's security by simulating a real-world, full-scale attack. While other tests might look for a "technical bug" in one app, the Red Team looks for a way to "win" by any means necessary.

Think of it as **A Professional Heist Crew hired by a Museum**:
*   The **[[blue-team|Blue Team]]** are the museum guards.
*   The Red Team doesn't just look for a broken window.
*   They might use **[[phishing|Phishing]]** to trick a janitor, wear a fake uniform to walk past the front desk, or hide inside the building after hours.
*   Their goal is to prove that "security" is often an illusion.

## The Art of the Simulation
Red Teams attack the **People, Processes, and Technology** all at once:
*   **Physical Breaches:** Can they walk into the server room by bringing "free donuts" to the receptionist?
*   **Social Engineering:** Can they trick the CEO into giving up their password over a "support" call?
*   **Stealth:** Once "inside," can they quietly stay there for weeks, stealing data without being noticed by the **[[soc|SOC]]**?

## Key Characteristics
*   **Adversarial Emulation:** They don't just use tools; they study how specific real-world groups (like **[[threat-actors|APT groups]]**) behave and copy their exact "style."
*   **Objective-Based:** They are given a goal, like *"Get the CEO's personal emails"* or *"Move $1 from the main vault,"* rather than just finding bugs.
*   **The Training Partner:** Their ultimate goal is to make the **[[blue-team|Blue Team]]** better. By being a realistic enemy, they ensure the defenders are sharp.

## FAQs

*1. Is it legal?*
**Absolutely.** It is a highly respected professional career. The difference between a Red Teamer and a criminal is **The Contract**. They have a legal document (often called a "Get Out of Jail Free" card) giving them explicit permission to attack the business.

*2. Red Team vs. Pen-Testing?*
**Pen-Testing** is a "check-up" for a specific app (e.g., *"Check if our website has SQL injection"*). **Red Teaming** is a full-scale war game (e.g., *"See if you can ruin our company using any method you want"*).
