# Shamoon

"The Burning of the Digital Oil Fields" ,a brutal, revenge-driven attack that didn't just steal data, but physically paralyzed the world’s richest oil company by "killing" 35,000 computers in a single afternoon.

**Shamoon** (2012) is one of the most aggressive **[[wipers|Wiper]]** attacks ever. It was used by state-sponsored hackers to attack **Saudi Aramco**. Unlike hackers who stay hidden to steal secrets, the people behind Shamoon wanted to be loud and destructive. They didn't just delete files; they replaced every hard drive with a picture of a **burning American flag**, then broke the computer so it couldn't turn on.

## The Chaos at Aramco
In the span of just a few hours:
*   **35,000 Computers** were turned into useless pieces of plastic and metal.
*   **Total Blackout:** Saudi Aramco (which manages $10\%$ of the world's oil) had to physically disconnect its entire company from the internet to stop the virus.
*   **Paper and Pencil:** For weeks, employees had to use typewriters and fax machines. Tanker trucks arriving to pick up oil had to fill out forms by hand because the digital pumps were dead.
*   **Bought the World's Hard Drives:** To fix the damage, Aramco reportedly bought every spare hard drive they could find on the global market, causing a worldwide shortage and price spike for computer parts.

## FAQs

*1. How did they get inside?*
**The Human Hole.** It is believed a single employee clicked a **[[phishing|Phishing]]** link or plugged in a malicious USB. Once inside that one computer, the virus used the worker's own "Keys" to spread to every other machine on the network.

*2. Why is this so famous?*
It was one of the first times we saw a nation-state use a cyberweapon to cause massive, real-world economic pain to a private company. It proved a "Digital War" could be just as expensive as a physical one.
