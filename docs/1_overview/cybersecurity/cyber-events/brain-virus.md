# Brain Virus

The Brain Virus is like a digital warning label placed over a book's cover, replacing the original title page to remind you not to read pirated copies, without actually tearing any pages out.

**Brain Virus** is the first widely known computer virus for the MS-DOS operating system and IBM PC platforms, developed in 1986.

Created by two Pakistani brothers to deter illegal copying of their medical software, the Brain Virus infected the boot sector of floppy disks. Instead of destroying data like modern malware, it simply attached itself to the disk's startup instructions.

When you put an infected floppy disk into a computer, it’s like putting a book onto a shelf that secretly places its own warning label onto any other books added to the same shelf. The computer would read the disk's startup instructions, unintentionally installing the virus in its memory. From there, the virus copied itself to any new, unprotected floppy disk inserted into the machine. 

## Key Characteristics

*   **Target:** Specifically infected the boot sector (the part telling the computer how to start) of 5.25-inch floppy disks.
*   **Stealth Tactics:** It was the first "stealth" virus. If a user tried to look at the infected boot sector, the virus would show them the original, clean version instead.
*   **Payload:** It didn't delete files. Its main action was displaying a message warning against pirated software and including the creators' contact information.

## FAQs

*1. Did the Brain Virus steal my data?*
No, it wasn't designed to steal or destroy data. Its main purpose was to track illegal copies of software and leave a warning message, though it could sometimes slow down the disk drive.

*2. How did it spread without the internet?*
It spread physically! Back in 1986, people shared files by handing floppy disks to one another. When an infected disk was placed into a new computer, the computer's memory became infected, which then passed the virus to the next clean floppy disk inserted.
