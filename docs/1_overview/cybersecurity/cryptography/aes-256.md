# AES-256

"The Unbreakable Glass Box" ,the gold standard for digital security. It is a mathematical fortress so strong that even if you used every computer on Earth to guess the password, it would still take trillions of years to get in.

**AES-256** is the "Advanced Encryption Standard" with a 256-bit key. It is used by the US government for "Top Secret" files, by banks for your money, and by your iPhone to protect your photos. It doesn't just "lock" a file; it shuffles the atoms of the data 14 times in a row using a vastly complex mathematical "recipe." 

Think of it like **A Vault with more combinations than there are atoms in the universe**:
*   **AES-128:** Very strong and "unbreakable" by today's standards.
*   **AES-256:** The "Overkill" version. It is specifically designed to be resistant even to the theoretical threat of future **[[quantum-computing|Quantum Computers]]**.
*   There are $2^{256}$ possible combinations. If a computer could check a billion keys per second, it would still outlive the universe before it found the right one.

## Key Characteristics
*   **Breathtaking Speed:** AES is a **[[symmetric-encryption|Symmetric]]** algorithm. It is incredibly fast, allowing your computer to encrypt and decrypt your entire hard drive in real-time without you ever noticing.
*   **Hardware Built-In:** Almost every modern computer chip (Intel, AMD, Apple) has a dedicated "AES Engine" physically built into the silicon, making the math instantaneous.
*   **No Backdoors:** Because it was chosen via an open, worldwide competition, it has been tested by thousands of experts for 20+ years. No one has ever found a way in.

## FAQs

*1. If AES is unbreakable, how do hackers steal my data?*
**They steal the KEY.** Hackers almost never try to break the AES math. Instead, they use **[[phishing|Phishing]]** to trick you into typing your password or **[[spywares|Spyware]]** to watch you do it. If a thief has your house key, even the strongest lock in the world can't save you.

*2. Does it drain my battery?*
**No.** Thanks to hardware acceleration (called **AES-NI**), your processor handles the math so efficiently that there is virtually no difference in battery life between an encrypted device and a normal one.
