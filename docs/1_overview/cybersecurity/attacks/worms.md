# Computer Worm

"The Self-Driver"—the ultimate digital parasite. It doesn't need you to click a link, download a file, or open an email. It simply finds a crack in the network and crawls inside on its own.

A **Computer Worm** is a standalone program that exists for one reason: to replicate itself across every computer on the network as fast as possible. While a **[[virus|Virus]]** needs to "Hitchhike" on a file you open, a Worm is "Self-Propelled." It scans the internet or your Wi-Fi looking for a security hole. When it finds one, it copies itself over, takes a breath, and starts looking for its next victim.

Think of it like **An Infectious Disease in a Crowded Room**:
*   A **[[virus|Virus]]** is like a poison on a doorknob—you have to touch it to get sick.
*   A **Worm** is like an airborne flu—you don't have to touch anything; you just have to be in the same room (on the same network) to catch it.

## Why are Worms so Dangerous?
1.  **Internet Speed:** Because no human action is required, worms can infect millions of devices worldwide in a matter of hours.
2.  **Bandwidth Hog:** Worms talk to thousands of computers at once to find new victims. This often crashes entire internet providers just by clogging up the digital "pipes."
3.  **The Delivery Man:** Most modern worms aren't just spreading for fun; they are carrying a "Payload"—like **[[ransomware|Ransomware]]** or a **[[botnets|Botnet]]** controller. 

## FAQs

*1. What was the "Morris Worm"?*
In 1988, a student wrote a simple program to see how big the internet was. It had a bug that made it replicate out of control, and it ended up crashing 10% of the entire internet in a few days. It was the world's first major wake-up call about digital security.

*2. How do I stop a worm?*
**Keep your software updated.** Worms almost always exploit "Known Holes" in your operating system. If you install your Windows or Mac updates the moment they appear, you "Patch the Leak," and the worm simply bounces off your computer.
