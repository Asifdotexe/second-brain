# Phishing

"Hacking the Human" ,the art of using digital manipulation to trick you into voluntarily handing over the keys to your own house.

**Phishing** is a type of attack where a scammer pretends to be someone you trust (your bank, Amazon, or even your boss) to steal your passwords or credit card numbers. Instead of a hacker spending months writing complex code to break through a company's firewall, they simply send a well-crafted email that says: *"Your account is locked! Click here to fix it."* Once you click and type your password, the hacker has won. They didn't "break in"; they were invited in.

Think of it like **A "Bait" Trap**:
*   **The Hook:** An urgent email or text message.
*   **The Bait:** A fake login page that looks exactly like Netflix or Gmail.
*   **The Catch:** You enter your info, and the hacker reels it in.

## The 3 Psychological Hooks
Phishing always uses your emotions against you:
1.  **Urgency:** *"Act in the next 10 minutes or your photos will be deleted!"* (Panic makes you stop thinking).
2.  **Authority:** *"This is the CEO. I need you to buy these gift cards immediately."* (We are wired to follow orders).
3.  **Curiosity:** *"Check out these leaked photos of you from last night!"* (Your ego is the "open window").

## FAQs

*1. How can I spot a "Phish"?*
Look for the **Red Flags**:
*   **Check the Sender:** Does the email claim to be "Apple" but the address is `support@apple-security-check-72.com`?
*   **The Hover Trick:** Don't click. Hover your mouse over the button. A box will show you where the link *actually* goes. If it's a mess of gibberish, it’s a scam.
*   **Bad Grammar:** Legitimate companies pay people to write perfect emails. Hackers often don't.

*2. I entered my password! What do I do right now?*
**Move Fast.** 
1.  **Change It:** Go to a safe device and change that password immediately.
2.  **Turn on MFA:** Enable Multi-Factor Authentication. Even if the hacker has your password, they can't get in without the code on your phone.
3.  **Scan:** Run an antivirus scan to make sure the site didn't install [[malware|Malware]] on your computer.
