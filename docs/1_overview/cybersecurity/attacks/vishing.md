# Vishing (Voice Phishing)

"The Digital Con Artist" ,a high-stakes scam where a hacker uses their voice, a fake caller ID, and a sense of panic to trick you into handing over your life savings.

**Vishing** is the more intense sibling of **[[phishing|Email Phishing]]**. While an email is cold and easy to ignore, a human voice on the phone can sound urgent, authoritative, and terrifying. The scammer might pretend to be from your bank's fraud department, the IRS, or even your company’s IT desk, using a believable script to make you feel like you are in trouble.

Think of it like **A "Phone Trap"**:
*   **The Setup:** Your phone rings, and it says "Chase Bank" or "The Police."
*   **The Hook:** *"We've detected a $2,000 charge on your account in Russia! We need to verify your ID to stop it."*
*   **The Catch:** They ask for your SSN, your PIN, or for you to "secure your money" by buying gift cards.

## How the Scammer Tricked You
1.  **ID Spoofing:** They use software to change their phone number to look like a legitimate company. You are 90% more likely to answer if the name is familiar.
2.  **Emotional Lever:** They create an immediate crisis. They don't want you to hang up and think; they want you to act while you are scared.
3.  **The Fake Solution:** They often ask you to download "remote support" software. Once you do, they have **[[root-access|Root Access]]** to your whole computer.

## FAQs

*1. How do I tell if a call is a "Vishing" scam?*
The #1 Golden Rule: **Legitimate organizations will NEVER call you and ask for your password, your PIN, or your security code.** If they ask for those, hang up. 

*2. But the Caller ID said "Apple Support"!*
It doesn't matter. Caller ID is like a return address on an envelope; any computer can lie about what it says. If you're worried, hang up and call the official support number listed on the company’s actual website.
