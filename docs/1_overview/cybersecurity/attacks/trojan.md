# Trojan Horse

"The Dirty Trick"—malicious software that disguises itself as a desirable gift (like a free game or a "crack") to trick you into letting it past your defenses.

A **Trojan Horse** is a type of **[[malware|malware]]** that wins through deception, not brute force. It is named after the ancient Greek myth where a wooden horse was given as a gift to the city of Troy. The citizens brought it inside their walls, only for the soldiers to sneak out at night and capture the city. In the digital world, the "Horse" is that "Free Minecraft Hack" or "Premium VPN Crack" you just downloaded.

Think of it like **A "Secret Backdoor"**:
*   **The Bait:** You see a "Free Movie Player" that looks legitimate and professional.
*   **The Invitation:** You click "Allow" on the security warning. You have just opened the city gates and bypassed your antivirus.
*   **The Payload:** While you're watching the movie, the Trojan is secretly installing a **[[spywares|Spyware]]** camera or turning your computer into a **[[botnets|Botnet]]** soldier.

## The Big Difference
Unlike a **[[worms|Worm]]** (which can crawl on its own) or a **[[virus|Virus]]** (which attaches to files), a Trojan **needs you**. It cannot infect your computer unless you specifically download it and hit "Run." It relies on "Social Engineering"—hacking the human, not the code.

## FAQs

*1. What is a "RAT"?*
Most modern Trojans are **Remote Access Trojans (RATs)**. They don't destory your files; they just create a secret tunnel that lets a hacker log into your PC whenever they want. They can watch your screen, use your webcam, and steal your files while you sleep.

*2. How can I stay safe?*
**Never download "Pirated" or "Cracked" software.** Scammers know that people looking for free stuff are likely to ignore security warnings. If a website offers you a $500 program for free, the program *is* the Trojan.
