# Wiper

"The Digital Shredder"—a brutal, irreversible type of malware that exists only to erase every single byte on a computer, leaving nothing behind but a useless pile of metal.

A **Wiper** has zero interest in profit. It doesn't want to steal your data, show you ads, or demand a ransom. Its only goal is **Destruction**. It is the "Suicide Mission" of the malware world. Once a Wiper runs, your photos, business records, and even the "brain" of your computer (the Operating System) are gone forever.

Think of it like **A Burglar Set on Fire**:
*   A **[[ransomware|Ransomware]]** hacker breaks into your house and locks your TV in a safe, offering to give you the key if you pay.
*   A **Wiper** hacker breaks into your house, sets the entire building on fire, and walks away.
*   There is no "backdoor." There is no "recovery." The data is simply destroyed.

## Why would someone use this?
1.  **[[cyber-warfare|Cyber Warfare]]:** Used by governments to cripple an enemy's banks, power grids, or military communications.
2.  **Evidence Sabotage:** Professional hackers often run a Wiper *after* they finish stealing data. They burn the crime scene to destroy the logs that would have helped the police track them down.
3.  **Pure Terror:** Some groups use them simply to humiliate a target by destroying their entire digital legacy.

## FAQs

*1. Was "NotPetya" a Wiper?*
**Yes.** In 2017, the **[[notpetya|NotPetya]]** attack looked like Ransomware—it asked for $300 to unlock your files. But researchers discovered the code was actually a Wiper; there was no way to unlock the files. It caused over $10 billion in damage worldwide.

*2. How do I survive a Wiper?*
You don't "clean" a Wiper after it hits; the damage is already done. The only defense is an **Offline Backup**. If your files are on a physical drive that isn't connected to your network, you can just buy a new computer and restore your life.
