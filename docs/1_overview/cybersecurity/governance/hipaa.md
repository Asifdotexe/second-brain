# HIPAA

"The Sacred Secret" ,a US federal law that ensures your most sensitive information ,surgeries, prescriptions, and mental health history ,stays strictly between you and your doctor.

**HIPAA** (1996) is the legal "firewall" around your medical life. In the US, it is a crime for a hospital, pharmacy, or insurance company to share your **Protected Health Information (PHI)** without permission. It ensures you can be 100% honest with your doctor, knowing your secrets are protected by federal law.

Think of your medical record as a **Biometric Vault**:
*   HIPAA dictates who has the key.
*   It sets the rules for how the vault must be built.
*   It punishes anyone who leaves the vault door unlocked.

## The Three Pillars
1.  **The Privacy Rule:** Sets the rules for who can talk about your health. A doctor can talk to your pharmacist, but never to your boss or the media without your consent.
2.  **The Security Rule:** The technical part. It requires hospitals to use **[[cryptography|Encryption]]**, firewalls, and strong passwords to protect your digital records from hackers.
3.  **The Breach Notification Rule:** If a hospital's database is hacked, they are legally required to tell you (and the government) as soon as possible. No hiding the results.

## FAQs

*1. Does it stop ME from talking?*
**No!** HIPAA only applies to "Covered Entities" (health pros and insurers). You own your data. You can post your own medical results on Facebook or tell your coworkers anything you want.

*2. How do companies "prove" they are safe?*
Since HIPAA is a broad law, many use a framework called **HITRUST**. It is like a "technical study guide" that helps hospitals ensure they meet all the law's requirements.
