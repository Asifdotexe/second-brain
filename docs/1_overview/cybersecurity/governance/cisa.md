# CISA (Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency)

"The Bodyguards of the Nation" ,a specialized US government agency that acts as the "Risk Advisor" for the entire country. They protect the power grids, water systems, and networks that allow society to function.

Founded in 2018, **CISA** is the coordinator between the government and private companies. In a world where a cyberattack can shut down a city's electricity or poison its water, CISA's job is to ensure the "Bad Guys" can't break the things we rely on to live.

Think of CISA as the **Neighborhood Watch for the Internet**:
*   **They don't arrest hackers** (that's the FBI's job).
*   **They don't spy on enemies** (that's the NSA's job).
*   **They find holes in the nation's armor** and tell everyone how to fix them.

## The Pillars of Protection
*   **Critical Infrastructure:** Working with electric, gas, and water companies to ensure their "Digital Front Doors" are locked.
*   **The KEV Catalog:** A "Wanted Poster" for bugs. It tells IT managers: *"These specific vulnerabilities are being used by hackers RIGHT NOW. Fix them today."*
*   **Election Security:** Ensuring that voting machines and election results are protected from foreign interference.
*   **Shields Up:** A call-to-action for companies to be on high alert during global tensions (like the start of a war).

## FAQs

*1. Does CISA protect my personal computer?*
**Indirectly, yes.** They provide free tools and advice for everyone. When CISA tells a major company like Microsoft to fix a bug, that fix eventually arrives on your computer as a **Windows Update**, keeping you safe.

*2. Is CISA part of the military?*
**No.** It is a civilian agency under the Department of Homeland Security. This is crucial because it allows them to work closely with private businesses that might be afraid to talk to the military or spies.
