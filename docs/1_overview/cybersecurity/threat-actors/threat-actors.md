# Threat Actors

Knowing your adversary.

To defend a network, you must first understand who is attacking it and why. This section profiles the different types of hackers, their motivations, and the techniques used by advanced adversaries.

## The Catalog of Threat Actors

### 1. [[hacker-types|Types of Hackers]]
The distinction between Authorized (White Hat), Semi-Authorized (Grey Hat), and Unauthorized (Black Hat) actors.

### 2. [[apt|Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs)]]
Sophisticated, long-term attacks often sponsored by nation-states for espionage or intellectual property theft.

### 3. [[insider-threats|Insider Threats]]
Security risks originating from within an organization, whether malicious or negligent.

### 4. [[ethical-hackers|Ethical Hackers]]
Security professionals who use hacking skills for defensive purposes.

### 5. [[hacktivism|Hacktivism]]
The use of digital tools for political protest, social change, or pursuing a cause.
