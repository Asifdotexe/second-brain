# Hacktivism

"The Digital Protest"—using hacking skills as a tool for social change, political activism, and civil disobedience. It is "activism" without ever leaving your bedroom.

**Hacktivism** is a mix of "Hacking" and "Activism." While a typical hacker wants money, a Hacktivist wants to send a **Message**. They use the internet to hold the powerful accountable, expose corruption, or protest injustice.

Think of it like **Digital Graffiti** or a **Digital Sit-In**:
*   **The Blockade:** In the real world, protesters might block a road; in the digital world, hacktivists use a **[[ddos|DDoS attack]]** to crash a website.
*   **The Billboard:** In the real world, people spray-paint a slogan; in the digital world, hacktivists "deface" a website's homepage to show their message.

## The Hacktivist's Toolkit
1.  **[[ddos|DDoS (The Digital Blockade)]]:** Flooding a website with so much "junk" traffic that it crashes. It’s the ultimate way to "shut down" a corporation for a day.
2.  **Doxing:** Finding a politician's private documents or a CEO's secret emails and posting them for the whole world to see. It’s meant to strip away the mask of the powerful.
3.  **Defacement:** Replacing a website’s homepage with a logo, a list of demands, or a political slogan.
4.  **Leaks:** Stealing massive databases (like the **Panama Papers**) to prove a government has been lying to the public.

## The Most Famous Group: Anonymous
Anonymous is the most well-known name in hacktivism. They have no leader and no headquarters; they are just a "brand" anyone can use to launch a protest. They are famous for their **Guy Fawkes Masks** and for taking on everyone from the Church of Scientology to the FBI.

## FAQs

*1. Is it legal?*
**No.** Even if your "reasons" are morally good, breaking into a website or launching a DDoS attack is a serious crime. Hacktivists can face years in prison for their protests.

*2. Are they "Heroes" or "Villains"?*
**It depends on which side you are on.** Supporters see them as modern-day Robin Hoods. Critics see them as "Cyber-Terrorists" who cause chaos and hurt innocent employees.
