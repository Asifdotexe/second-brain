# Unit Bias

"Finish What You Start" ,the psychological urge to finish an entire "serving" or "object" simply because it was handed to you as one piece, regardless of how much you actually need.

**Unit Bias** is the reason you eat the whole bag of chips even if you weren't that hungry. Our brains hate "Unfinished Business." We don't rely on our bodies to tell us when we are full; we rely on **Visual Clues**. When we see a plate, a bag, or a bottle, our brain defines that as "One Unit." If we don't finish it, our brain feels like we "failed" a task.

Think of it like **Building a Lego Set**:
*   You open a small box of Legos. You spend 10 minutes and you're happy when it's done.
*   Someone hands you a **Giant Bucket** of Legos. You spend 5 hours building it. You are tired, your back hurts, and you want to stop. 
*   **The Bias:** You keep building because you want to "Finish the Bag." Your brain treats the bag as a "Unit" of work that **must** be completed.

## Portion Distortion
This is the main cause of overeating in modern society.
*   **The 32oz Soda:** To your brain, this is "One Drink."
*   **The 8oz Soda:** To your brain, this is also "One Drink."
*   **The Result:** You will drink the whole 32oz and feel just as "satisfied" as if you had drank the 8oz, simply because in both cases, you "Finished the Unit."

## FAQs

*1. Does this apply to work too?*
**Yes!** If someone gives you a list of 100 emails to answer, it feels impossible. Your brain doesn't see a "Unit" it can finish. But if you take 5 emails and put them in a separate folder, your brain sees that folder as **"One Task."** You will work much faster because the "Finish Line" is visible.

*2. How can I use this to be healthier?*
**Smaller Units.**
*   Use a smaller plate. Your brain will see a "Full Plate" (one unit) and feel satisfied. 
*   Buy individually wrapped snacks instead of a family-sized bag. Your brain will "Stop" after one small bag because it has checked the "Done" box.
