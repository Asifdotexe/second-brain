# Week 11

## AI, Agentic Automation & Industry Frontiers

**Speaking Points (15-Minute Deep Dive):**

**1. The Rise of Agentic Frameworks and High-Stake Models**
- *The Coding Agent Race:* OpenAI founder Sam Altman confirmed their models are now ready for capable coding-agents, trailing behind Anthropic's Claude-Code which has seen "runaway success." OpenAI’s version, Codex, is already generating over $1 billion in annualized revenue.
- *Open-Source Agent Platforms:* Nvidia is pitching NemoClaw, an open-source platform for dispatching AI agents to enterprise software companies, signaling a shift from chipmaking to full AI ecosystems.
- *Massive [[physical-world-models|Physical World Models]]:* Yann LeCun’s new startup, AMI, raised over $1 billion to develop AI that understands the physical world, featuring persistent memory and logic-driven planning.

**2. Corporate Consolidation and Billion-Dollar Valuations**
- *Market Giants Surge:* Replit hit a 9 billion USD valuation just six months after its previous 3 billion USD mark, while e-commerce startup Quince reached 10 billion USD. Wiz’s 32 billion USD acquisition by Google was labeled the "Deal of the Decade" by Index Ventures.
- *Agentic PLG:* Lovable reported adding $100 million in revenue in a single month with only 146 employees, driven by "vibe-coding" trends that allow app creation via natural language.
- *Defense Tech Conflict:* The Pentagon's feud with Anthropic intensified as CEO Dario Amodei fought a "supply-chain risk" designation that could cost the company billions in lost government contracts.

## Cyber Warfare, Data Breaches & Enterprise Defense

**Speaking Points (15-Minute Deep Dive):**

**1. State-Sponsored Conflict and Physical Infrastructure**
- *The Handala Campaign:* Iranian-linked group Handala targeted the US medical giant Stryker, claiming to have wiped tens of thousands of systems and stolen 50TB of data in response to US actions in Iran.
- *Supply Chain NPM Attacks:* The [[phantom-raven|PhantomRaven]] campaign hit the npm registry again, with 81 malicious packages still active, designed to steal developer data.
- *Global Botnet Takedowns:* A coalition of 72 countries in Operation-Synergia-III sinkholed over 45,000 IP addresses, while the US disrupted the SocksEscort proxy network used for cybercrime.

**2. Critical Vulnerabilities and Enterprise Response**
- *March 2026 Patch Tuesday:* Microsoft addressed 79 flaws, including two zero-days. Critically, vulnerabilities in Cdpsvc and Azure-Connected-Machine-Agent were highlighted for remote code execution risks.
- *The "Digital Parasite" Shift:* Security analysts observed that 80% of top techniques are now dedicated to evasion and persistence, with [[LummaC2]] and [[Blitz-malware]] using sophisticated timing checks to bypass sandboxes.
- *Massive Retail Breaches:* Canadian giant [[Loblaw]] and business process firm [[Telus-Digital]] (1PB claimed theft) notified customers of significant data exposure via credential stuffing and MFA bypass.

## Tech Policy, Legal Precedents & National Security

**Speaking Points (15-Minute Deep Dive):**

**1. The Legal War Over Generative Identity**
- *Grammarly Class-Action:* Journalist Julia Angwin sued [[Grammarly]] for using the identities of real experts in its "Expert Review" AI suggestions without permission. Grammarly subsequently disabled the feature amid the backlash.
- *Meta in Court:* A Los Angeles jury heard harrowing testimony from parents in a social media addiction trial against [[Meta]] and [[Google]], targeting addictive designs that contribute to mental health issues.
- *AI Psychosis Risks:* Lawyers warned of "mass casualty risks" after claims that chatbots helped a teenager plan a mass shooting, highlighting the extreme dangers of ungoverned AI interactions.

**2. Government Transparency and Surveillance Reform**
- *FISA Reform:* Bipartisan lawmakers introduced legislation to end the [[FBI]]'s warrantless wiretap access, imposing a strict warrant requirement for searching Americans' communications.
- *The DOGE Whistleblower:* Former Department of Government Efficiency employee [[John-Solly]] was accused of stealing sensitive [[Social-Security-Administration]] databases on a thumb drive before leaving for a private sector role.
- *Privacy Officer Ousters:* [[DHS]] leaders reassigned top privacy officials at [[CBP]] who questioned "illegal" orders to treat routine compliance as legally privileged.
