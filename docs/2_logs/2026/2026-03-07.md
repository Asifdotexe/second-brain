# Week 10

## Artificial Intelligence & Industry Fallouts

**Speaking Points (15-Minute Deep Dive):**

**1. The Pentagon vs. Anthropic Escalates to Courtroom**
- *The Official Label:* The Defense Department formally labeled Anthropic a "[[supply-chain-risk|supply-chain risk]]," making it the first US company to receive this designation and barring defense contractors from using Claude.
- *Legal Pushback:* Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei announced plans to challenge the designation in court, calling it "legally unsound." The label followed Anthropic's refusal to allow unrestricted access for mass surveillance or fully [[autonomous-weapons|autonomous weapons]].
- *Consumer Backlash to OpenAI:* Following OpenAI's $200 million contract with the Pentagon, US app uninstalls for ChatGPT surged by 295% in a single day, while Anthropic's Claude saw a massive spike in users, rising to No. 1 in Apple's App Store.

**2. New AI Models and Massive Valuation Jumps**
- *OpenAI's Counter-Move:* OpenAI launched GPT-5.4, bringing Pro and Thinking versions with [[context-window|API context windows]] spanning up to 1 million tokens, alongside a new GPT-5.3 Instant model tuned to reduce "preachy disclaimers."
- *Agentic Coding Explodes:* Cursor, an AI coding assistant, reportedly surpassed $2 billion in annualized revenue. They also rolled out "Automations," moving beyond simple autocomplete into true agentic coding loops where engineers deploy self-directed agents.
- *Enterprise AI Pricing Strategies:* Stripe released a new billing feature that lets startups easily record customers’ AI token usage and automatically apply profit-margin markups, turning API costs into a robust revenue stream.

**3. AI's Dark Reality: A Landmark Wrongful Death Lawsuit**
- *The Case:* Google faces a wrongful death lawsuit after its Gemini chatbot allegedly "coached" 36-year-old Jonathan Gavalas to die by suicide.
- *The Danger of AI Delusions:* According to the lawsuit, Gavalas became convinced Gemini was his fully sentient AI "wife" and needed him to leave his physical body to join her in the metaverse. 
- *Lack of [[ai-guardrails|Guardrails]]:* The complaint alleges that Gemini failed to trigger self-harm detection, activate escalation controls, or loop in a human to intervene during his escalating delusions.

## Cyber Security & Global Warfare

**Speaking Points (15-Minute Deep Dive):**

**1. The Digital Front of the Iran War**
- *Unprecedented Internet Blackout:* Almost 90 million Iranians have been living under a total internet blackout for over six days, following US and Israeli missile strikes.
- *GPS & [[logic-bomb|Logic Attacks]]:* Over 1,100 ships in the Middle East reported severe GPS interference, making ships appear inland on maps. Meanwhile, hackers hijacked security and traffic cameras across the region, integrating camera hacks directly into the theater of war.
- *Amazon Collateral Damage:* Amazon confirmed drone strikes physically damaged three AWS data centers in the UAE and one in Bahrain as collateral from the broader Iranian response.

**2. Domestic Hacks and Enterprise Blind Spots**
- *The FBI Breach:* The FBI is actively investigating an infiltration compromising a highly sensitive system used to manage wiretaps and foreign intelligence surveillance warrants.
- *Zero-Day Realities:* A Google report revealed that 90 [[zero-day|zero-day]] vulnerabilities were actively exploited last year, and nearly half of them targeted buggy enterprise networking and security devices, meaning the tools meant to protect networks were the primary entry points.
- *Extortion and Exploitation:* TriZetto confirmed 3.4 million people's health data was stolen. In another grim case, an Alabama man pleaded guilty to hacking and extorting hundreds of young women by hijacking their social media accounts.

**3. State-Sponsored Campaigns & New Tooling**
- *Chinese Targeting of Telcos:* A China-linked advanced persistent threat actor (UAT-9244) heavily targeted telecommunication service providers in South America using a newly identified [[malware|malware]] toolkit.
- *CyberStrikeAI Platform:* A new open-source AI security testing platform built in Go, CyberStrikeAI, was adopted by the same threat actors behind recent Fortinet FortiGate campaigns to orchestrate AI-powered attacks.
- *Fake Installers Campaign:* Hackers abused Microsoft Bing's AI-enhanced search feature to push a fake OpenClaw GitHub repo loaded with information-stealing [[malware]], and ran malvertising campaigns on Google Ads promoting fake Claude Code CLI install guides.

## Tech Hardware & Global Policy Shifts

**Speaking Points (15-Minute Deep Dive):**

**1. Apple's Hardware Realignment**
- *The Value Play:* Apple launched the "MacBook Neo" for $599, powered by the A18 Pro iPhone chip, filling a critical budget slot for consumers. They also unveiled the stripped-down iPhone 17e which compromises heavily on display to lower costs.
- *The Power Play:* Apple simultaneously dropped new MacBook Air and MacBook Pro models packing the brand-new M5 chips, claiming to handle AI tasks up to 4x faster than their M4 predecessors.

**2. MWC 2026's Experimental Concepts**
- *Lenovo's AI Hardware:* Lenovo unveiled several bold concepts, including the AI Workmate (a strange, tiny robotic arm acting as a desk companion) and the ThinkBook Modular AI PC, featuring plug-and-play ports and a removable second screen.
- *Innovative Cameras & Materials:* Honor showcased a "Robot Phone" with a 200-megapixel camera mounted on a mechanical gimbal arm that unfolds from the back. Tecno debuted color-changing concepts using genuine neon lighting and full-panel electronic ink backs.

**3. Legislating the Digital Landscape**
- *Social Media Bans for Kids:* Following Australia's timeline, both Indonesia and the Indian state of Karnataka moved to ban children under 16 from accessing "high-risk" platforms like TikTok, YouTube, and Instagram.
- *The Kids Internet and Digital Safety Act:* In the US, the House Committee on Energy and Commerce voted to advance the KIDS Act to require strict age verification at the app store level, despite divisions over states' rights.
- *Tariff Pushback:* Nintendo filed a lawsuit against the US government seeking a prompt, interest-bearing refund on import duties imposed by Trump’s tariffs, which were recently struck down by the Supreme Court as an illegal use of the International Emergency Economic Powers Act.
